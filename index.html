AI Programs

Closing the AI gap for progressives

The question for the progressive ecosystem is no longer whether AI will reshape democracy — it will. The question is whether the people who care most about democratic health will help shape how.

The movements that adopt these tools effectively in the next 12–18 months will have structural advantages that compound into 2028 and beyond. HGI is building the programs to make that happen.

Monthly

AI Open Mics

Monthly virtual convenings where practitioners bring real, specific needs: voter targeting, message testing, social media data extraction, agentic workflow automation, campaign data security, and AI applications for democracy and human rights.

Bi-weekly

Claude Code Study Halls

Entry-level, hands-on sessions for practitioners who have never written a line of code. Participants build working tools in two hours. A field organizer spending four hours manually pulling data could automate that in thirty minutes — they just don't know it yet.

Ongoing

Salon Discussions

Small-group convenings focused on specific projects and challenges, designed for the kind of focused, cross-pollinating conversation that doesn't happen in larger formats. Recent topics include Generative Engine Optimization (GEO).

Launching 2026

AI Fellows Program

A cohort-based program building practitioner expertise and thought leadership. Fellows will hold domains including: agentic AI in organizing, tool evaluation, organizational AI policy, vibe coding, use case development, and defensive applications.

Ongoing

Speaking & Events

HGI staff speak at convenings across the progressive tech and organizing ecosystem, bringing AI literacy and strategic framing to practitioner audiences wherever they gather.

2025 Media Innovation Fellows

Six projects reimagining progressive media

Selected from over 100 proposals, our inaugural fellows are building tools and playbooks at the intersection of media, technology, and organizing.

Laura Liibbe

WhatsApp Channels Playbook

A playbook for using WhatsApp Channels as high-trust infrastructure to engage Latino and immigrant audiences where they already congregate.

Andrew Markoff

M3 Media Planning Platform

Cross-channel media optimization helping campaigns allocate budgets more effectively for voter persuasion and turnout.

Steven McAlpine

INTRVL Creator Audience Profiling

Political and behavioral insights into creator audiences, helping advocacy campaigns measure and target their creator partnerships.

Jesse Thomas

Project Shoutout

Testing sponsorships with livestream creators on YouTube, Twitch, and Kick to drive measurable civic engagement actions.

Liz Utrup

Real People Project

Online ethnography research uncovering how real voters engage with digital content, informing smarter outreach strategies.

Gabriella Zutrau

Instagram Chatbot Playbook

Recommendations for social media chatbot usage in politics and advocacy, turning online enthusiasm into real-world action.

Each fellow receives a $15,000 grant, mentorship, coaching, and showcase opportunities.
